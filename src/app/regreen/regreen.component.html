Project overview

In 2019, during our project for the Management of Change course at University, we had the chance to meet with the CEO of La Vie - Nestlé Waters Vietnam. The company is committing to turn 100% of its packaging recyclable or reusable by 2025.

The problem that they are facing is how to recycle water bottles more effectively. In Vietnam, there is no formal system for collecting recyclable waste like papers, metals,... All these jobs are done by informal services called “Ve chai”. Scrap collectors would travel around aimlessly by bicycle and looking for sellers.

Competitor analysis



The gap

  • Price is fixed on competitors’ apps but is rather low-priced. There is no app with free price communication and suggested market price available.
  • Donate option is not available.
  • The competitors don’t have clear gamification reward system to encourage users to use more of the app.
Project goals

Main features

From the interviews and competitor research of current online services, I was able to find the main goals the app can focus on

Connect better and faster

Connect seller with scrap collectors within a few taps on the phone. Users can choose to sell immediately, or set schedule. People who have little to sell can find other sellers in the vicinity to sell together.

Sell or donate if you like

Some users don’t like saving up used stuff at home. They can set schedule for collectors to come get it, and donate all or a portion of the money and transfer them into point.

Gamification Based Reward

Clear rewarding system for users. Encourage them to sell more by creating communities where friends can do challenges and compete together.

Wireframes and wireflows
Notable improvements

1. “Selling products” Interaction

Before

To sell products, users can press and hold each item to know more about product information. After that, they can tap to choose desired products.

Although there’s a description, 5/5 users are not aware of that.

After

Users can tap each item to know more about product information. Navigation buttons (Close tab, Add another product, Sell now) are placed on the product card.

2. Step-by-step information filling

Before

Users have to type in the product weight. All CTA buttons (Sell with others, Schedule, Sell now) are placed in one place.

4/5 users are confused about the CTA buttons

“I only understand the “Sell now” button. I don’t understand what “Sell with others” and “Schedule” means, so I skipped them.”

After

Divide into smaller steps and add description below each option for users to understand.

Users type in the weight first, the app brings them to the next step of selling options available for each type of weight users have.

What can be improved

New feature

One of our concerns while building the app is how to help scrap collectors, who are not tech-savvy, to easily use the service.

As for another edge case where scrap collectors don’t use smartphones, confirmation page will be improved. Order details can also be adjusted by users after scrap collectors' assessment. Then they can use a PIN number from scrap collector’s phone to confirm the last number detail.
